Packaged Meals Data Analysis

For the 2021 UMD data challenge, my team chose to analyze a dataset provided by the USDA. The data consisted of over 4400 rows of food and ingredient information. We first did some surface-level cleaning using OpenRefine. Then we used Python to further clean the data by removing parentheses and brackets. Finally, we used the Python matplotlib library to create bar charts for data visualization. What we discovered was that a lot of the top ingredients in these packaged foods are preservatives, additives, or stabilizers. In addition, we determined that dairy/egg-based products seem to be the healthiest meal category due to the high amount of vitamins/minerals and low amount of preservatives. Salt, water, and sugar were found in the highest number of ingredient combinations. Soy and its derivatives were found to be very popular ingredients. As a result, we suggested a focus on the soy industry supply chain as soy is critical not only as a food ingredient but also as a feed ingredient in the meat industry.
